Which is Better - Cats or Dogs ?

Disclaimer: All statistics and facts on this website are made up, and untrue.

Introduction

For many years, it has been a heated debate on whether a cat is a better pet, or a dog is a better pet. Usually, the discussion comes down to the overall circumstance and situation that the potential owner is in. This discussion also can be compared with many different pros and cons for each animal which can be found here.

Statistics

Once again, please note that all the information on this page is false and untrue. According to information from surveys taken in early 2023, there is a big difference in what people want based on their current finacial situation and age. Surveys show that kids aged from 4 - 10 showed a much greater interest dogs rather than cats. These numbers were 76% in favour of dogs, and the other 24% in favour of cats. The age group of 11 - 17 was also surveyed, and their results were very close, these numbers being 58% in favour of dogs, and the other 42% in favour of cats. This data shows us a lot with regards to the different situations that people are in, and the next survey further proves this point. The final two surveys that were taken, were from groups aged 18 - 40, and 50+. Both groups showed much stronger interest in owning cats rather than dogs. Surveys showed that 63% of adults aged from 18 - 40 showed an interest in cats, and the other 37% showed a greater interest in dogs. The final survey (aged 50+) were in extreme favour for cats. This number being a whopping 82% in favour of cats, and the remaining 18% being in favour of dogs. For a further, and in depth look into the statistics, please click here to visit our second page.

Analysis

Based on this information we can conclude that as the general population gets older, there is a higher demand for cats over dogs. This can be explained by the time needed to train and maintain dogs rather than cats, and this can be seen in the next section Advantages and Disadvantages.

Advantages and Disadvantages

Below you can see what local experts list as the pros and cons for each animal.

Dogs

  • Pros
    1. Very intelligent
    2. Extremely playful
    3. Loyal (levels based on type of breed)
    4. Very cute
  • Cons
    1. Can be very noisy (levels once again, based on type of breed)
    2. Sometimes can be extremely energetic
    3. Sometimes can be hard to train
    4. Takes up a lot of your time (especially in the early years)

Cats

  • Pros
    1. Very cute
    2. Usually can manage themselves (don't need to be walked, etc.)
    3. Very playful
    4. Can help remove a lot of stress
  • Cons
    1. Sometimes can be too energetic
    2. Usually cannot be trained (if they are, it takes a lot of time)
    3. Have very sharp claws
    4. Outdoor cats are prone to fleas, and other diseases

Obviously, both animals have the same pros and cons, but these are just few of the many examples that have been recorded by the experts.
